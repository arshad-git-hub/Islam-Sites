Shahadat : Onnes of God ( Allah )

The concept of the Shahadat or Shahadah—the testimony of faith—is the very foundation of Islam. It begins with the declaration: “La ilaha illallah” — “There is no god but Allah.” This statement affirms the absolute oneness of Allah (Tawheed), which is the core belief that distinguishes Islam from all other religions.

Allah is One, indivisible, and without partner. He has no children, no parents, and no equal. He alone created the universe and sustains it. Nothing can exist or function without His will. The Qur’an emphasizes this in many verses, such as in Surah Al-Ikhlas: “Say: He is Allah, the One. Allah, the Eternal. He begets not, nor was He begotten. And there is none like unto Him.”
(Qur’an 112:1–4)

Belief in the oneness of Allah shapes every aspect of a Muslim’s life—from worship to daily actions. Muslims turn only to Allah in prayer, seek His guidance, and place full trust in Him. Associating others with Allah (shirk) is considered the gravest sin in Islam. The Shahadat not only declares belief but is a lifelong commitment to worship and live for Allah alone, recognizing Him as the sole authority and sustainer.
